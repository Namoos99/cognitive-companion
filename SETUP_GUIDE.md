# Setting Up Daily Companion — A Guide for Families and Caregivers

Daily Companion is a free, gentle five-minute daily activity for the older
adult in your life. This guide walks you through setting it up on their
phone, tablet, or computer. **No technical experience needed** — most
families finish setup in under five minutes.

## The one thing to know first

**The app works completely without any setup or account.** Just opening
the website is enough. Everything below is about making it *easier to
reach* and, optionally, giving it fresh daily variety.

The app's link is:

**https://namoos99.github.io/cognitive-companion/**

All information (check-ins, exercise results, journal entries) stays
privately on the device itself. Nothing is uploaded anywhere, and there
are no accounts, passwords, emails, or ads.

## Step 1 — Put it on the home screen (recommended)

This makes Daily Companion open with one tap, like a regular app, instead
of typing a web address every day.

**On an iPhone or iPad:**
1. Open **Safari** and go to the app's link above.
2. Tap the **Share button** — the square with an arrow pointing up, at the
   bottom of the screen (or top on iPad).
3. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**.
4. Tap **Add** in the top corner. Done — a "Daily Companion" icon now sits
   on the home screen.

**On an Android phone or tablet:**
1. Open **Chrome** and go to the app's link above.
2. Tap the **three dots** in the top-right corner.
3. Tap **Add to Home screen**, then **Add**.

**On a computer:** simply bookmark the page, or drag the address onto the
desktop to make a shortcut.

## Step 2 — Adjust the text size together

Open the app and look for the **A+ larger** button in the top-right
corner. Tap it and ask which size is more comfortable. The app remembers
the choice.

## Step 3 (optional) — Fresh daily exercises from Claude

Out of the box, the app rotates through a built-in set of exercises.
If you'd like each day's words, categories, and prompts to be freshly
generated by Claude (an AI assistant made by Anthropic), you can add an
API key. Think of it as a small prepaid account that the app uses once
per day.

**What it costs:** the app makes one tiny request per day. This works out
to a few cents per month — a $5 credit purchase lasts a very long time.

**Who does this step:** you (the family member or caregiver), once, on
the device the older adult uses. They never need to see or touch any of
this.

1. On your own computer, go to **console.anthropic.com** and create an
   account (this is Anthropic's developer site — separate from the
   claude.ai chat app).
2. Follow the prompts to verify your email, then open **Billing** and add
   a payment method or purchase a small amount of credits ($5 is plenty).
   Tip: set a monthly spending limit here for peace of mind.
3. Open the **API Keys** section and click **Create Key**. Name it
   anything (for example, "daily-companion") and copy the key — it looks
   like `sk-ant-...` and is only shown once.
4. On the older adult's device, open Daily Companion, tap **Settings**,
   paste the key into the box, and tap **Save settings**.

That's it. From tomorrow onward, each day's session content will be
freshly generated. The key is stored only on that device and is used
only to fetch the day's exercises. If the internet is down or anything
goes wrong, the app quietly falls back to its built-in exercises — a
session is never blocked.

**If the key ever stops working** (for example, credits run out), nothing
breaks — the app simply returns to its built-in exercise bank until you
top up or replace the key.

## A few tips for building the habit

- **Pair it with an anchor:** right after breakfast or with the morning
  coffee works well. Same time, same place.
- **Do the first few sessions together.** Sit alongside, let them do the
  tapping.
- **Rest days are fine.** The progress view treats missed days kindly on
  purpose. Encouragement works better than streak pressure.
- **The reflection step can be spoken.** Writing is optional — thinking or
  telling you the memory out loud counts just as much.

## An important note

Daily Companion is a wellness activity, not a medical test, and it cannot
detect or diagnose any condition. Changes in scores day to day are normal
and expected. If you have concerns about a loved one's memory, the right
step is a conversation with their doctor. In the US, the Alzheimer's
Association helpline (1-800-272-3900) is available 24/7 for families.
